# Situational Strength as a Lens to Understand the Strain Implications of Extra-Normative Work
**Authors:** Charles Calderwood, Rustin D. Meyer, Molly E. Minnen (2023)

## Core Theoretical Contributions
- **Extra-Normative Work:** Introduces the construct of [[Extra_Normative_Work]], which captures work arrangements where effort is required in ways, at times, and/or from locations that differ substantially from typical expectations.
- **Faceted Strain Implications of Situational Strength:** Theorizes and demonstrates that the four facets of [[Situational_Strength]] (clarity, consistency, consequences, constraints) have diverging effects on employee strain.

## Methodology
Three multi-modal studies including an experimental vignette (Study 1), a survey on after-hours work (Study 2), and a survey on COVID-19 telework (Study 3).

## Core Empirical Findings
- **Diverging Facet-Level Effects:**
  - **Consistency and Clarity:** Higher *consistency* and *clarity* were associated with *lower* emotional exhaustion, work anxiety, and perceived job stress.
  - **Consequences and Constraints:** Perceiving severe *consequences* or *constraints* was associated with *higher* emotional exhaustion, work anxiety, and job stress.
