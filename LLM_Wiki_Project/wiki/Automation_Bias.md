# Automation Bias

Automation bias occurs when human users become overly reliant on the outputs of automated systems or AI, leading to errors when the AI fails. It is a critical risk factor in human-AI collaboration.

## Overreliance Outside Capabilities
Research by Dell'Acqua et al. (2026) in the domain of [[AI_and_Work]] highlights the dangers of "falling asleep at the wheel." When faced with a complex task outside the AI's "jagged technological frontier," subjects using GPT-4 were 19 percentage points less likely to produce the correct solution than those without AI. Despite being highly skilled, the workers overly relied on the AI's plausible but flawed analysis rather than synthesizing the nuanced data themselves.
