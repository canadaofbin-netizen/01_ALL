# Steady-State Visual Evoked Potentials (SSVEP)

Steady-State Visual Evoked Potentials (SSVEP) are signals that are natural responses to visual stimulation at specific frequencies. When a person looks at a flickering light, the visual cortex generates electrical activity at the same (or multiples of) the flickering frequency.

## High-Frequency SSVEP in AR/VR
The highly controllable micro-displays in modern AR/VR headsets (capable of 360Hz refresh rates) are ideal for SSVEP-based [[Brain_Computer_Interface]] paradigms. Leveraging high-frequency, imperceptible flickers maximizes the Information Transfer Rate (ITR) while simultaneously eliminating the visual fatigue and photosensitive discomfort typically associated with lower-frequency SSVEP stimuli.
