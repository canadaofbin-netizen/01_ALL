# Cold Email Strategy

Cold emailing is a critical tool for bypassing automated ATS filters and securing niche research or engineering internships.

## Core Principles
1. **Establish Common Ground**: Always highlight shared affiliations (e.g., UCL alumni, shared academic disciplines like linguistics or psychology).
2. **Demonstrate Unique Value**: Instead of generic enthusiasm, pitch a specific, highly relevant project. For BCI, this means framing side projects around the company's specific form-factors (e.g., proposing lateral EEG decoding for smart glasses).
3. **Prove Engineering Rigor**: Explicitly mention experience with complex data pipelines and automated data collection to prove practical utility.
4. **The Soft Ask**: Request a brief 15-20 minute 'coffee chat' or advice rather than directly asking for a job, reducing the barrier to response.

## Academic Outreach (e.g., University Professors)
- **Focus**: Express gratitude for past interactions, update them on current research (e.g., meta-analysis data pipelines), and ask for networking advice or connections to industry labs (e.g., UCLIC, ICN).

## Industry Leaders Outreach
- **Focus**: Connect their successful productization experience (e.g., real-time conversational AI) with current BCI challenges (processing noisy, streaming biometric data). Ask high-level architectural questions regarding ultra-low latency and context window optimization.

## Company Recruiters & Researchers
- **Focus**: Bypassing ATS by emailing HR/Talent teams or direct researchers. Emphasize the intersection of Human Factors/Cognitive Science and raw data engineering.
