# University of Birmingham

## Background
The University of Birmingham is a major UK research university. It houses the Centre for Human Brain Health (CHBH) within the School of Psychology, which hosts world-class neuroimaging and neurostimulation facilities.

## Key Attributes
Home to the [[Kornysheva_Lab]] (Skilled Action and Memory Lab), which conducts cutting-edge research on motor sequence planning using the [[Competitive_Queuing]] model and non-invasive [[Brain_Computer_Interface]] technologies for motor disorder rehabilitation. The lab recently secured a £2.1M UKRI Future Leaders Fellowship and ARIA Opportunity Seed funding for [[Scalable_Neural_Interfaces]] research in collaboration with Oxford University.

The CHBH's equipment infrastructure includes MRI, conventional MEG, OPM-MEG, Focused Ultrasound Stimulation (FUS), TMS, fNIRS, and high-density [[Electroencephalography]] — encompassing nearly all existing non-invasive neuroscience equipment.
