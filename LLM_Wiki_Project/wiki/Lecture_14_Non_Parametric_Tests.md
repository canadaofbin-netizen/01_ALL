# Lecture 14: Non-parametric Tests

## Overview
Non-parametric tests make weaker assumptions than parametric tests (like t-tests and ANOVAs) and do not assume data are drawn from a specific normal distribution. They often work by ranking the raw scores.
*(Source: Lecture 14. Non-parametric tests - notes.pdf)*

## When to use them
- Data violate normality assumption.
- Outliers are present.
- Focus on medians rather than means.
- Small sample sizes.

## Common Tests
- **Wilcoxon rank-sum test (Mann-Whitney U)**: Alternative to independent t-test.
- **Wilcoxon signed-rank test**: Alternative to paired t-test.
- **Kruskal-Wallis test**: Alternative to One-way ANOVA.
- **Spearman's Rank**: Alternative to Pearson correlation.

While robust, non-parametric tests are generally less powerful than their parametric equivalents when assumptions are met.
