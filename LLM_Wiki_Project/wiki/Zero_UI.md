# Zero-UI

Zero-UI (Zero User Interface) is the ultimate design paradigm in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) where the interface becomes entirely transparent and invisible. Users interact with technology through natural, innate behaviors—such as thought, subtle gaze, or micro-gestures—without relying on traditional screens, physical controllers, or explicit command inputs.

## Achieving Zero-UI in Spatial Computing
In the realm of AR smart glasses, true Zero-UI is the holy grail. As outlined in the [[BCI_Roadmap]], traditional gaze-only interfaces fail to achieve Zero-UI because they suffer from the "Midas Touch" problem (unintended activations). By implementing [[Asymmetric_Hybrid_Control]] (e.g., fusing EOG for navigation with EEG/EMG for instant click intent), systems can provide a flawless, frictionless interaction experience that feels telepathic, dismantling the physical and cognitive barriers of conventional interfaces.
