# Counterproductive Work Behavior

Counterproductive Work Behavior (CWB) refers to voluntary acts by employees that harm the organization (e.g., sabotage, theft) or its members (e.g., incivility, bullying).

## Predictors and Moderators
- **Behavioral Displacement**: When organizational constraints (strong situations focused on task performance) successfully block poor task performance, employees who are predisposed to low performance may experience elevated negative affect. Dalal et al. (2020) found that this frustration leads to behavioral displacement. Employees redirect their negative tendencies into CWB as an alternative "outlet," causing a mismatch in [[Situational_Strength]] expectations.
- **Robot Exposure**: Yam et al. (2023) demonstrated that heightened [[Job_Insecurity]] triggered by robot exposure can mediate increases in burnout and workplace incivility (a form of interpersonal CWB).
