# Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier

## Theoretical Contributions
This paper introduces the concept of a **"jagged technological frontier"** to describe the uneven and often counterintuitive landscape of AI capabilities in the domain of [[AI_and_Work]]. Within the frontier, AI acts as a powerful complement to human work (a "booster"), while outside the frontier, it can degrade human performance (a "disruptor") as workers may become overly reliant on plausible but incorrect AI outputs, leading to [[Automation_Bias]]. 

## Methodology
The authors conducted a preregistered, randomized laboratory-in-the-field experiment with 758 highly skilled management consultants from Boston Consulting Group (BCG) using GPT-4.

## Core Empirical Findings
- **Inside the Frontier (Performance Booster)**: Consultants using AI significantly outperformed those in the control group. They completed 12.2% more tasks, finished 25.1% faster, and produced work of significantly higher quality.
- **Skill Equalizer**: AI disproportionately benefited lower-performing consultants.
- **Outside the Frontier (Quality Disruptor)**: For the task outside AI's capabilities, consultants using AI were 19 percentage points *less likely* to produce the correct solution compared to the control group due to overreliance and [[Automation_Bias]].
