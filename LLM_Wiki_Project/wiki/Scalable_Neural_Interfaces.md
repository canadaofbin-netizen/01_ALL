# Scalable Neural Interfaces

## Definition
Scalable Neural Interfaces is a research paradigm and specific ARIA-funded project that aims to construct massive multi-modal foundation datasets by combining neural signals — collected via high-density [[Electroencephalography]] (EEG) and magnetoencephalography (MEG) — with precise kinematic behavioral data of the hand and arm. This complex data is then fed into state-of-the-art multi-modal AI, specifically GPT-based foundation models, to decode intricate hand and arm movement intentions at high resolution.

## Key Details

### ARIA Opportunity Seed Funding
The project was initiated in September 2025 with "Opportunity Seed" funding from the UK's Advanced Research and Invention Agency (ARIA). It is led by the [[Kornysheva_Lab]] at [[University_of_Birmingham]] in collaboration with:
- Professors Oiwi Parker Jones and Ole Jensen from Oxford University
- Dr. Diar Abdlkarim from the University of Birmingham

### Core Architecture
1. Construct a massive multi-modal foundation dataset combining high-density EEG and MEG neural signals with precise kinematic behavioral data.
2. Feed this data into GPT-based foundation models to decode intricate hand and arm movement intentions.
3. The fusion of generative AI and non-invasive brain signals is expected to break through the accuracy bottlenecks of traditional motor cortex signal decoding.

### Clinical Application
The goal is to provide an innovative neuroprosthetic control system capable of generating scalable and safe rehabilitation commands for a wide range of motor disorder patients, including stroke and Parkinson's disease patients. This directly builds on the [[Competitive_Queuing]] model's insight that motor rehabilitation must target sequence planning of individual actions.

### Cambridge NeuroWorks Ecosystem
Scalable Neural Interfaces is also a key focus area of [[Cambridge_NeuroWorks]], whose multi-million pound fellowship ecosystem was explicitly created to accelerate the clinical application and commercialization of scalable neural interfaces for intractable brain diseases (depression, dementia, chronic pain, phantom limb pain, stroke, Parkinson's). The [[Kornysheva_Lab]] operates within both the ARIA-funded academic research sphere and this broader clinical commercialization ecosystem.
