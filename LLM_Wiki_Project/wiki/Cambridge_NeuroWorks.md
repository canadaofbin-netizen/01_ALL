# Cambridge NeuroWorks

## Background
Cambridge NeuroWorks is a massive neurotechnology innovation community and funding ecosystem launched as part of the Activation Partner program supported by the UK government's Advanced Research and Invention Agency (ARIA). Multiple core institutions have united: the University of Cambridge, Babraham Research Campus, Maxwell Centre, and Milner Therapeutics Institute. The goal is to accelerate the clinical application and commercialization of [[Scalable_Neural_Interfaces]] capable of addressing intractable brain diseases such as depression, dementia, chronic pain, phantom limb pain, stroke, and Parkinson's disease.

## Key Attributes

### Three-Tier Fellowship Model ("Innovation Funnel")
While traditional academia is often intolerant of failure, this ecosystem strongly supports the entrepreneurial spirit of "fail fast and retry."

1. **What If Program** (Part-time Ideation): Scientists, engineers, and clinicians invest ~4 hours/week to refine unconventional neurotech hypotheses. Includes virtual mentoring, business basics education, and venture creation strategies. Culminates in a pitch deck reviewed by VC experts.
2. **Blue Sky Fellowship** (12-month Full-time PoC): After a 3-month scoping and hypothesis formulation phase, fellows receive strong financial and technical support for 9 months to conduct a Proof of Concept. Provides financial freedom to completely scrap an existing hypothesis and start over.
3. **Frontier Fellowship** (Clinical Scaling & Commercialization): For "Doers" who scale technologies that have completed a successful PoC. Support for health economics modeling, regulatory approval preparation, and formative studies incorporating patient feedback to achieve a successful spin-out.

### Networking & Community Value
- Hosts annual flagship "NeuroWorks" conference (e.g., June 2026 at Cavendish Laboratory, University of Cambridge).
- Hub where top UK neurotech scholars, clinical specialists (e.g., Harith Akram, neurosurgeon at UCLH), venture capitalists, and regulatory experts interact daily.
- Synergies prioritizing "collaboration over competition" frequently occur through community events — finding paper co-authors or recruiting core startup team members.
- Subscribing to their newsletter and participating in events provides a macro-perspective of UK [[Brain_Computer_Interface]] trends and access to hidden employment opportunities.
