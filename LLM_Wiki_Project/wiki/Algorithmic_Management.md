# Algorithmic Management

Algorithmic management involves using software algorithms to continuously track, evaluate, and direct the behavior of human workers, often replacing traditional human supervisors.

## Organizational Impact
As explored in the [[Kim_Lab_Journal_Club_Summer_2026]] under the broader theme of [[AI_and_Work]], algorithmic management raises critical questions about worker autonomy and control. It can:
- Manufacture consent through constant and confined choices.
- Alter perceptions of fairness and benevolence, often triggering [[Algorithm_Aversion]].
- Potentially act as a modern form of [[Situational_Strength]], enforcing strict behavioral norms and restricting variance in employee actions.
