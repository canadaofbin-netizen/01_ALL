# The Research Process

The empirical [[Research_Process]] is the foundation of modern psychological and scientific research. It transforms subjective observations into testable, objective findings through a standardized series of steps.

## The 8-Step Process
1. **Choose a research question**: Originated from an observation.
2. **Select variables**: 
   - **Independent Variable (IV / Predictor)**: The variable that does the explaining or is manipulated.
   - **Dependent Variable (DV / Outcome)**: The variable being explained or measured.
3. **Operationalisation**: Transforming abstract, theoretical constructs into concrete and measurable variables (e.g., turning "sleep" into "hours slept"). There are always multiple ways to operationalise a variable.
4. **State your hypothesis**: A testable belief about the outcome of the study formulated in terms of operationalised variables. (See [[Hypothesis_Testing]])
5. **Select a sample**: Choosing a representative **sample** from the broader **population** of interest. (See [[Sampling]])
6. **Collect data**: Standardizing the collection into usable formats and identifying missing or erroneous data. See [[Variables_and_Measurement]] for data types.
7. **Analyse data**: Using [[Descriptive_Statistics]] (summarising data) and inferential tests like [[T_Test]] and [[Correlation]] (testing hypotheses). Visualise data as well (see [[Data_Visualization]]).
8. **Draw conclusions**: Conclude if the data supports the hypothesis. One study cannot support a hypothesis conclusively due to potential measurement or sampling limitations.
