# Extra-Normative Work

Introduced by Calderwood et al. (2023), *extra-normative work* refers to work arrangements requiring effort in ways, at times, or from locations that differ substantially from an employee's typical expectations (e.g., after-hours work, abrupt teleworking). 

## Strain Implications
How this work is communicated dictates its impact on employee strain via the facets of [[Situational_Strength]]. Conveying extra-normative work with high clarity and consistency mitigates strain, whereas imposing severe consequences or constraints exacerbates emotional exhaustion and work anxiety.
