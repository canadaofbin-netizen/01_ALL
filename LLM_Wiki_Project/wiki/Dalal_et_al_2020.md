# Extending Situational Strength Theory to Account for Situation-Outcome Mismatch (Dalal et al., 2020)

## Core Empirical Findings
- **Replication of Anomalous Findings**: A strong situation focused on task performance actually *strengthens* rather than weakens the impact of personality traits on [[Counterproductive_Work_Behavior]] (CWB).
- **Mediation via Negative Affect**: The strengthening effect of task-performance-focused situational strength on the personality-CWB relationship is mediated by negative affect. 
- **Attenuation via Matched Situational Strength**: CWB-focused situational strength successfully weakened the relationship between personality traits and CWB. 

## Theoretical Contributions
- **Situation-Outcome Match**: Extends traditional [[Situational_Strength]] theory by proposing that the theory's primary prediction (strong situations constrain personality expression) only applies when there is a match between the situational cues and the behavioral outcome domain.
- **Behavioral Displacement**: Introduces the idea that when strong situations block one outlet for low performance, individuals redirect their poor performance to an alternative outlet (e.g., CWB) that is not currently blocked.
