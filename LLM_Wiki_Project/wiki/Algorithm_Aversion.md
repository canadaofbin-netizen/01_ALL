# Algorithm Aversion

Algorithm aversion refers to the phenomenon where humans erroneously avoid or distrust algorithms, particularly after seeing them make a mistake, even if the algorithm outperforms human judgment on average. This is often contrasted with "Algorithm appreciation," where people prefer algorithmic to human judgment.

## Context in AI and Work
In the context of [[AI_and_Work]], the [[Kim_Lab_Journal_Club_Summer_2026]] highlights research exploring when people trust AI versus humans. Key debates revolve around:
- The "Transparency Dilemma": whether disclosing AI use erodes trust.
- Perceptions of Benevolence: whether being managed by AI feels less benevolent than human management (related to [[Algorithmic_Management]]).
- Capability-Personalization frameworks and individual differences that determine if aversion or appreciation wins out.
