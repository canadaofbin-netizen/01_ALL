We conducted a one-way ANOV A on running speed (km/hr) with Group 
(three levels: High Tempo, Low Tempo, and No Music) as a between-
subject variable. There was a significant main effect of group, F(2, 27) = 
10.52, p < .001, η2 = .44. 
Post-hoc comparisons using Bonferroni correction showed that running 
speed was significantly higher in the High Tempo music group (M = 
11.13, SD = 0.96) compared to the Low Tempo music group (M = 8.53, 
SD = 1.23, p < .001) and the No Music group (M = 9.56, SD = 1.57, p = 
.031). The Low Tempo and No Music group did not differ significantly in 
running performance (p = .248).Write-up checklist
• Report type of ANOVA performed (“… one-way ANOVA …”)
• Report the DV and IV (including levels) (“… speed (km/hr) with Group (three 
levels: High Tempo, Low Tempo, and No Music) as a between-subject variable 
…”) 
• Comment on significance (“… There was a significant main effect of group …”)
• Report F(df) = Fvalue, p=pvalue (“…F(2, 27) = 10.52, p < .001 …”)
• Report effect size (eta squared) (“η2 = .44”)
• Report post-hoc comparisons and adjusted p-values (“… Post-hoc comparisons 
using Bonferroni correction showed that …”)
• Describe the direction of the pairwise comparisons (“…  running speed was 
significantly higher in the High Tempo music group compared to the Low 
Tempo music group …”)
• Report exact p-values, unless p<.001
• Report all values to 2 decimal places (except for p values)Visualise the data
