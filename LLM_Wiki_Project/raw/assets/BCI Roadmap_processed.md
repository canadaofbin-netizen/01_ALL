# **📄 \[Research Proposal / Detailed Report\]**

## **1\. Title**

* **Beyond the Spatial Horizon: An Asymmetric Hybrid Biosignal (EOG-EEG-EMG) Interface for All-in-One Smart Glasses**

## **2\. Introduction & Research Background**

**2.1 The Bottleneck of Spatial Computing Interfaces and the Form-Factor Dilemma**

In the era of next-generation spatial computing, Augmented Reality (AR) smart glasses are rapidly emerging as the core platform for continuous, everyday digital interaction. However, in stark contrast to the exponential advancements in visual output technologies, input interfaces suffer from a severe bottleneck. Voice recognition induces privacy concerns and social friction in public spaces, while optical camera-based hand-tracking forces the user's hands to remain within the camera's limited field of view (FoV), inevitably causing cognitive and physical fatigue (e.g., the "Gorilla Arm" syndrome).

**2.2 Confronting the Physical Limits of EEG: Volume Conduction and the Loss of Spatial Resolution**

To overcome these limitations, non-invasive Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs) that transparently decode latent cognitive intent have gained traction as the ultimate alternative. Yet, **temporal EEG sensors embedded in the temples of smart glasses face a fatal anatomical limitation: an extreme loss of spatial resolution due to the "Volume Conduction" effect.** Because minute electrical signals generated in the cerebral cortex scatter and blur as they pass through the cerebrospinal fluid and the skull, it is physically impossible to decode precise directional intent (e.g., flicking a single thumb "left" vs. "right") using only the limited channels available on an eyewear frame.

**2.3 A Clear Comparison with Meta’s Wristband (HD-EMG) and the Imperative for an All-in-One Form Factor**

Recently, Meta proposed high-density electromyography (HD-EMG) wristbands to bypass this directional limitation. Forearm muscles are large and distinctly grouped, allowing the system to precisely map the electrical topography of subtle finger movements. However, this introduces a fundamental flaw: **"Form-Factor Fragmentation," requiring the user to wear a mandatory secondary device on their wrist.** True ambient computing is only realized when interactions are entirely self-contained within a **single 'All-in-One' smart glasses form factor** (Hands-free / Wrist-free), without relying on auxiliary peripherals.

**2.4 Proposed Solution: Asymmetric Hybrid Control**

Abandoning the academic dogma of attempting to resolve both spatial navigation (2D direction) and execution (click) through a single EEG modality, this study proposes a paradigm-shifting **"Asymmetric Hybrid Biosignal AI."** Because temporal EEG cannot resolve spatial direction, we embrace this physical limit and assign the Degrees of Freedom (DoF) asymmetrically: **Electrooculography (EOG / subtle eye movements) is allocated for 2D spatial navigation (Direction), while EEG combined with facial EMG (jaw muscle tension) generated by micro-gestures is strictly allocated to confirm the user's intent (Click/Activation).** This elegant circumvention of physical limitations provides a flawless Zero-UI experience, marking a breakthrough in the spatial HCI paradigm.

## **3\. Research Objectives & Framework**

The ultimate objective of this research is to **"construct a highly robust (\>90% accuracy) hybrid control system applicable in everyday environments, using solely the sensors embedded within a smart glasses form factor, without any wrist-worn peripherals."** To achieve this, we execute a meticulous four-phase research roadmap, transitioning from controlled laboratory settings (*In-vitro*) to dynamic real-world ecosystems (*In-the-wild*).

* **Study 1 (Defining Engineering Limits):** Quantifying the signal degradation caused by form-factor reduction (64ch $\\rightarrow$ 4ch) and recovering the practical accuracy threshold via extreme engineering tuning.  
* **Study 2 (The UX Paradigm Shift):** Abandoning the impossible task of directional decoding, focusing instead on extracting a flawless 'Binary Click' intent with minimized cognitive load via 'Micro-gestures'.  
* **Study 3 (The AI Fusion Breakthrough):** Fusing EOG (Navigation) \+ EEG/EMG (Activation) modalities and maximizing HCI usability based on Fitts' Law.  
* **Study 4 (Ecological Validity Verification):** Proving real-world robustness by deploying IMU-based adaptive noise cancellation against dynamic motion artifacts (walking/talking) *In-the-wild*.

## **4\. Detailed Study Design (Study 1–4)**

### **🔬 Study 1\. Verifying Temporal Limits and Engineering Accuracy Rescue (*In-vitro*)**

* **Objective:** To quantify the catastrophic drop in classification accuracy (Degradation) when a high-density 64-channel EEG cap is reduced to a smart glasses form factor (4 temporal channels: T7, T8, TP7, TP8), and to rescue the performance back to the practical BCI threshold (70%) through rigorous engineering optimization.  
* **Experimental Design & Analysis:**  
  * **Dataset:** PhysioNet 64-channel EEG Motor Imagery Data.  
  * **Degradation Phase:** Utilizing Recursive Feature Elimination (RFE) to systematically exclude the central motor cortex signals (C3, Cz, C4), scientifically quantifying the vertical drop in accuracy due to the loss of spatial resolution.  
  * **Rescue Engineering:** Reclaiming the 70% threshold through temporal-specific optimizations, including 10\~24Hz band-pass fine-tuning, Common Spatial Pattern (CSP) component reduction, and Support Vector Machine (SVM) regularization (Grid Search).  
* **➡️ Logical Bridge:** *Through extreme engineering, we successfully rescued the accuracy to 70%, yet it remains far below the commercial standard (95%). More critically, the user suffers immense cognitive load from maintaining absolute physical stillness for "Pure Motor Imagery (MI)." Thus, we must cease squeezing the algorithm and fundamentally redesign the User Experience (UX).*

### **🔬 Study 2\. UX Optimization: Abandoning Directionality for Binary Click Extraction**

* **Objective:** To boldly abandon the anatomically impossible task of decoding left/right directional finger movements from the temporal lobe, and to identify the optimal UX condition for extracting a flawless 'Binary Click' intent with minimal fatigue.  
* **Hypothesis & Neuroscientific Rationale:** Compared to Pure MI (which lacks physical feedback), performing a sub-centimeter "Micro-gesture" triggers proprioceptive feedback and efference copies, which will exponentially amplify the Event-Related Desynchronization (ERD) power in the motor cortex while drastically reducing user fatigue.  
* **Metrics & Evaluation:**  
  * **Dataset (Newly Acquired):** A custom pilot dataset (N=15) comprising Class 0 (Rest), Class 1 (Pure MI), and Class 2 (Micro-gesture \< 1cm).  
  * **Metric 1 (Cognitive Ergonomics):** Quantifying the reduction in fatigue using the **NASA-TLX (Task Load Index)** and physiological EEG fatigue biomarkers (Alpha/Theta power ratio).  
  * **Metric 2 (Signal Processing):** Binary classification accuracy (Target \>85%) and ERD power spectrum mapping.  
* **➡️ Logical Bridge:** *Micro-gestures halved cognitive fatigue while securing a highly reliable 'Left-Click' mechanism with over 85% accuracy. However, to master 2D spatial computing, a 'Mouse Cursor' (directionality) is mandatory. It is time to transform the historically discarded facial noise signals into our most powerful fused modality.*

### **🔬 Study 3\. Multi-modal AI Fusion (The Hybrid BCI Breakthrough)**

* **Objective:** To surpass the 95% commercial accuracy standard by integrating simultaneously acquired EOG (subtle ocular tremors/gaze direction) and EEG+facial EMG (micro-gesture click intent & jaw tension) into a single deep learning fusion network on a temporal form factor.  
* **Experimental Design (Integration of Fitts' Law):**  
  * **Hybrid AI Architecture:** Constructing a CNN+LSTM multi-modal network (Late Fusion strategy) designed to synchronize and learn the spatiotemporal patterns of raw temporal signals across different modalities.  
  * **HCI Usability Verification:** Moving beyond simple classification accuracy by implementing **'Fitts' Law' (ISO 9241-9 multi-directional tapping test)**, the gold standard for HCI usability evaluation.  
    * *Control Group:* Conventional Eye-tracking UI utilizing Dwell-time activation.  
    * *Experimental Group:* Asymmetric Hybrid Control (EOG for navigation \+ EEG/EMG micro-gesture for instant click).  
  * **Evaluation Metrics:** Deep learning accuracy (\>95%), **Throughput (bits/s)**, and Target Acquisition Time. This phase will empirically prove the definitive resolution of the "Midas Touch" (unintended activations) problem, a chronic flaw of gaze-only systems.  
* **➡️ Logical Bridge:** *The Fitts' Law experiment proved overwhelming performance and usability in a controlled environment, achieving the ultimate Zero-UI. But users in reality walk down streets and converse over coffee. Can this delicate system withstand the massive mechanical noise of an 'In-the-wild' ecosystem?*

### **🔬 Study 4\. Ecological Validity Verification (*In-the-wild* Validation)**

* **Objective:** To prove the system's robustness by actively controlling massive mechanical and physiological artifacts generated in dynamic, everyday environments (Walking, Talking) outside the laboratory.  
* **Experimental Design & Defense Logic:**  
  * **In-the-wild Scenario:** Subjects wearing wireless temporal prototypes perform smartphone scrolling/clicking missions under three conditions: 1\) Sitting baseline, 2\) Walking on a treadmill at 4 km/h, and 3\) Reading text aloud (Talking).  
  * **IMU-based Adaptive Noise Canceling:** The 1\~3Hz heel-strike impacts and triboelectric friction noise entirely mask biological signals during locomotion. To resolve this, we utilize the **smart glasses' built-in IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit) kinematic data as a noise reference signal. By applying Adaptive Filtering (NLMS/RLS algorithms), we dynamically subtract this mechanical noise in real-time.**  
  * **Evaluation Metrics:** Maintaining \>90% control accuracy under extreme real-world noise, pre/post-subtraction Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR), and real-time processing latency (\< 300ms).
