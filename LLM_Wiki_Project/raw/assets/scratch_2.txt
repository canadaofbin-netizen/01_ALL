χ2 as a ‘goodness of fit’ test  
• Does the observed data ‘fit’ our expectations if the null hypothesis were true?  
• Example research question: Are births equally distributed across seasons?  
• Data (number of births per season):  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 What is the likelihood for the observed count of births for each season, if 
equal proportions of births are expected? 
Hypotheses and expected frequencies 
• If births occurred with equal frequency, the probability of births in each season 
would be 0.25 (1/4) 
• To obtain the expected count frequencies for each season, we divide the total 
number of births by the number of seasons:  
4700 ÷ 4 = 1175 
or 
4700 × 0.25 = 1175 
• H0 = births occur with equal frequency across seasons  
o P = (.25, .25, .25, .25) 
• H1 = births do not occur with equal frequency across seasons  
o P ≠ (.25, .25, .25, .25) 
 
The χ2 statistic (chi2, pronounced “kai squared”) expresses the difference between 
Observed and Expected frequencies:

Calculating χ2 
 
𝜒2 distribution for the null hypothesis 
• Normal variables = the expected frequencies that would be observed if there 
were no association between the categorical variables being studied. 
• Sum of squared differences that are normally distributed yield a χ2 distribution 
• χ2 is always positive 
• df is the number of k groups/categories being added together minus 1 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Finding the critical χ2 value – anything larger than this will be too unlikely to result 
from sampling error , therefore we can reject H0:  
➔ For the χ2-distribution with 3 degrees of freedom 
and an alpha level of p = .05, the critical χ2-value is 
7.81.  
Our χ2-value 15.7 > critical value 7.81 ,  so we can 
reject the null hypothesis.

Reporting the chi-square test of independence 
 
Assumptions of the test: 
• The data in the cells should be frequencies, or counts of cases  
• The levels (or categories) of the variables are mutually exclusive.  
• Each subject may contribute data to one and only one cell.  
• The study groups must be independent and not related.  
• There are 2 variables, and both are measured as categories. 
• The expected cell frequencies should be greater than 5.
